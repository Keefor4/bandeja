## Design Context

### Users
Padel match reviewers — coaches, video editors, or club staff — working at a desk reviewing full-match footage. The review console is used for extended sessions (30–90 min), so the interface must be low-fatigue and keyboard-optimised. Tablet use courtside is possible (admin + reviewer roles).

### Brand Personality
Athletic precision, cinematic focus, expert tooling. Three words: **Precise. Athletic. Focused.**

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm Cinematic Dark** — not the cold blue-black of generic AI SaaS. Charcoal-neutral backgrounds with a subtle warm undertone. The signature accent is **Electric Lime** (`#B8FF40`) — the colour of a padel ball under court spotlights. It's unexpected in a professional tool context, which makes it instantly memorable.

- **Anti-references**: cyan-on-dark AI dashboards, neon-glow SaaS, glassmorphism-heavy layouts
- **References**: DaVinci Resolve colour grading panels, premium sports analytics platforms, Bloomberg Terminal meets Criterion Collection packaging
- **Theme**: Dark only (video review requires low ambient light)
- **Typography**: Plus Jakarta Sans (headings + body) + DM Mono (monospace code/timestamps)
- **Logo mark**: Padel racket (oval head with perforations + short handle) — replaces the generic search/scope icon

### Design Principles
1. **The UI recedes, the video leads.** Interface chrome should be dark and quiet so reviewer attention stays on the footage.
2. **Keyboard-first, mouse-friendly.** Every core action has a shortcut. Hover states and visual affordances support mouse use without requiring it.
3. **Precision over decoration.** No glow effects for the sake of it. Every visual element earns its place.
4. **Warm neutrals, not cold blues.** Backgrounds use `#09090A` (warm-neutral near-black), not blue-tinted darks. Text is `#F0EDE9` (warm white).
5. **Electric lime is the only accent.** No secondary accent colour competing for attention. Green/red/amber/purple are reserved for status signals only.
